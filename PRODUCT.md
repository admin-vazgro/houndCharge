# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

HNI and angel investors in India — typically 35–55, managing personal capital or family wealth, already familiar with equities and real estate, actively seeking differentiated alternative assets. They browse on desktop for deep research and on mobile for first impressions. They are sceptical of gimmicks; they respond to exclusivity, precision, and scarcity framing.

## Product Purpose

Hound Charge is India's first investable EV charging asset network. The landing page converts investor curiosity into early-access sign-ups for Project Ares — a strategic high-speed charging ring around Ernakulam's major entry corridors. Success = a prospective investor sends the "invest@houndcharge.com" email or requests the investor brief.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Bold. Pioneering.

Tone: confident without arrogance, premium without stiffness. Think a private equity deck crossed with an architectural monograph — numbers matter, but so does vision.

Emotional goal: leave the investor feeling *genuinely curious and slightly FOMO* — this is new, it is real, and being early matters.

## Anti-references

- Generic Indian fintech (Groww / Zerodha colour language — teal/green on white, consumer-casual)
- SaaS-cream landing pages (Notion / Linear aesthetic — too soft and product-y for a hard-asset investment)
- Government infrastructure portals (PSU/IPO brochure style — institutional but not premium)
- Crypto / Web3 neon glassmorphism (speculative feeling undercuts credibility)
- Do NOT change the existing visual theme: dark black backgrounds, gold (#c9ad7d) accents, Manrope + Space Grotesk + Playfair Display type system.

## Design Principles

1. **Scarcity is the message** — every section should communicate that this is limited, early, and real — not a vague future promise.
2. **Numbers anchor trust** — data points (13 hubs, 65 chargers, 120 kW, ₹5L minimum) are credibility devices; give them space and weight.
3. **Dark canvas, gold signal** — the black background is intentional: it creates the premium, night-drive feeling of EV infrastructure. Gold is used sparingly as a signal, never decoration.
4. **Motion serves orientation** — animations reveal the *scale* and *progression* of the network, not just add polish. Every animation should answer "so what?"
5. **Mobile = first impression** — HNI investors will first encounter this on a phone. The mobile experience should feel as intentional as the desktop, not a scaled-down afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA for text contrast (critical: gold-on-black combinations must meet 3:1 for large text, 4.5:1 for body)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all scroll-driven and entrance animations
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px on all interactive elements
- Font sizes: 16px minimum for body copy on mobile
